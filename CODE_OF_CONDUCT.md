# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

JOC (JayOnCode) is committed to a respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free environment for everyone who participates — contributors, maintainers, users asking questions, and anyone engaging in project spaces.

We pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Show empathy, patience, and professionalism
- Assume good intent while staying clear and constructive
- Give feedback about ideas, designs, and code — not about people
- Respect different backgrounds, experience levels, and communication styles
- Accept responsibility when mistakes happen, and learn from them
- Focus on what is best for the community and long-term health of the project
- Credit others’ work and avoid taking credit for contributions you did not make

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or threats
- Personal attacks, insults, or hostile language (including coded insults)
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Publishing others’ private information (physical or electronic) without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, pull requests, or community spaces
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and when an individual is representing the project in public spaces. Examples include:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and code review
- Documentation comments and docs site feedback channels
- Official project chat, email, or social accounts (when used for project business)
- Events or meetings where someone is representing JOC

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, report it **privately** to the maintainers. Do not escalate publicly in issues or pull requests when privacy matters.

**Preferred contact:** email [jayoncode@gmail.com](mailto:jayoncode@gmail.com) with subject line starting with `[CoC]`.

Include as much relevant detail as you can (what happened, where, when, and any links). Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with care. Maintainers will respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

For **security vulnerabilities** in published packages or repository tooling, use [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) instead of this channel.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

- Warning the person involved
- Editing or deleting comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions
- Temporary or permanent bans from project spaces
- Reporting to the platform (e.g. GitHub) when policy or law requires it

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by project leadership.
